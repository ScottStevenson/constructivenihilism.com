Constructive Nihilism

An open-source religion founded on truth and pragmaticism

Version 0.1 - open for feedback

  1. Life has no meaning or purpose

    • There is no fundamental axiom we can derive meaning or purpose from. "Should" logic cannot be derived from the "is" logic, which we've uncovered through observation, science, math and logic.
    • This is clearly visible in meditation.
  2. All experiences, sensations and events are neutral
    • Because life has no purpose, there is no reference context with which we can judge our experiences as "good" or "bad". We cannot even say that pain is bad, or pleasure is good.
    • This is also plainly visible when viewing reality clearly through meditation.
  3. Most of the time, most people, are unable to "access" the truths of (1) and (2). We are lost in artificial layers of reality created by other humans and our own genetic coding.
